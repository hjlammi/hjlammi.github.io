Thoughts and plans

When trying to learn coding concepts, I think it is important to try to understand what you are doing instead of simply copying stuff from Stack Overflow. In addition to the how something is done, I'm also curious about why something is done the way it is done.

At this level of competence I think my strengths are in Front-End Development since that is what I have practised the most. Usability of an app and responsive design are naturally essential factors in front-end development. I'm also interested in the visual aspect of a product. Accessibility is something that I would like to learn more about.

Even though I'm still in the beginning of my career I'm always interested in learning more and developing my skills further. In the spring term I will take courses in, for example, database programming, web programming and Haskell, after which I'm convinced I will know enough to be able to move on to perform well and learn more in real-life working environment. It is not a problem for me to have to learn new skills (i've basically done it my whole life!) which is essential in this line of work.

In near future I want to keep learning more about React. For example, component lifecycles are still a bit of a mystery to me: when to use what and why. Redux library would be nice to take into use as well. I've also started learning Clojure but due to more urgent stuff to learn, the project is on hold for now. I'd also like to build a chatbot and my own version of a Twitter-kind-of-a messaging service with bots tweeting content. There are so many cool things to learn and so little time!
